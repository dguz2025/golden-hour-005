Two machines, two questions

Google ranks pages. AI cites passages.

Google — a crawler army
“Is your page the best answer?”
  • Follows links, builds one index of pages
  • A link is a vote — a popularity contest
  • The unit is your whole page
AI — a pattern machine
“Do trustworthy sources agree on who you are?”
  • Chunks your page — retrieves passages, not sites
  • Two clocks: citations = live retrieval, days; memory = training, months
  • Cites the sources it trusts — rarely you
Ahrefs · 15,000 prompts — on average, only ~12% of AI-cited URLs ranked in Google’s top 10 for the same question

You can be page one on Google and invisible in ChatGPT.

The mental model

Two gates. They multiply.

Gate 1 — Entity
“Do you exist to the machine?”
  • In the records machines treat as ground truth — Wikidata first
  • different from splits your name-twin; sameAs asserts who you are

Gate 2 — Content
“Can it lift a clean answer?”
  • The question as the heading
  • A 40–60 word self-contained answer right under it
  • Facts up top — structure beats length

Press without entity is noise. Entity without press is silence.

Save your money

Three things to stop buying.

01 · The old scoreboard
The number isn’t the work.
  • Chasing the DA score — the number, not the work
  • llms.txt files — no measured effect
02 · Press before entity
Noise without a name.
  • 11 fake experts · 600+ real articles
  • Zero recommendations — across 9 models
  • You can’t trick it. You can only show it.
03 · Black boxes
Confidence isn’t proof.
  • $50K agencies · $400 dashboards
  • “Ask for their validation data”
  • Most can’t show it — go human
The meta-stop: stop looking for a trick — the gameable era is the one that just ended.

Nobody’s a ten-year expert in a two-year-old field.

Read your logs

Three bots at your door. One gets you cited.

01 · Training
Bakes you in.
  • Library acquisition — a copy for the next retrain
  • Months out — and weights can’t emit a link
  • GPTBot · ClaudeBot · CCBot
02 · Search index
Makes you citable.
  • The card catalog — always crawling
  • The citation lane — never block these
  • OAI-SearchBot · PerplexityBot · Claude-SearchBot
03 · User fetch
Quotes you once.
  • The errand runner — one page for one live answer
  • Per-conversation — it doesn’t index you
  • ChatGPT-User · Perplexity-User
A fetch in your logs is supply, not a citation — and GA4 can’t see any of it: crawl truth lives only in your server logs.

Your logs prove the bot came — not that you got chosen.

The harness
  • 5 AIs — ChatGPT, Google AI, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude — incognito
  • 2 kinds of questions — “who is [you]?” and “best [category] for [use case]”
  • ~5 runs each — the answers are stochastic; read the pattern, not one run

  • Recommended?
